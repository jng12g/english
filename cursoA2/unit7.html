En la unidad anterior empezaste el pasado con was/were y verbos regulares. Ahora añadimos los irregular verbs, las dates y los ordinal numbers para contar pequeñas historias de vida con más naturalidad.

By the end of this unit, I can…

Section B · Grammar 1

Past simple: irregular verbs

Los verbos irregulares no terminan en -ed. Hay que aprender su forma de pasado: go → went, buy → bought, see → saw.

Rule

Affirmative past simple

En afirmativa, el verbo cambia a su forma de pasado. La forma es igual para todas las personas: I went, she went, they went.

Base verbPast simpleExample
gowentShe went to London.
havehadThey had a small flat.
meetmetHe met his friend in 2019.
buyboughtWe bought a car.
writewroteMy aunt wrote a book.
Typical Spanish error

No añadas -ed a un verbo irregular: She goed to school. → She went to school.

Rule

Negative and questions

Con didn't y Did...?, el verbo principal vuelve a la forma base: She didn't go, Did she go?

TypePatternExample
+subject + past formThey bought a flat.
−subject + didn't + base verbThey didn't buy a house.
?Did + subject + base verb?Did they buy a flat?
Typical Spanish error

Después de didn't o Did, no uses el pasado: She didn't went. → She didn't go.

Section C · Grammar 2

Dates and ordinal numbers

Para hablar de biografías necesitas fechas. En inglés, los días del mes se dicen con números ordinales: first, second, third.

NumberOrdinalShort form
1first1st
2second2nd
3third3rd
4fourth4th
5fifth5th
12twelfth12th
20twentieth20th
21twenty-first21st
Rule

Dates: in or on?

Usa in con años y meses: in 1998, in June. Usa on con fechas concretas: on 3 May, on my birthday.

Good to know

Al leer una fecha como 3 May, puedes decir the third of May o May the third. Para A2, reconoce ambas.

Typical Spanish error

Con una fecha exacta no uses in: I was born in 3 May. → I was born on 3 May.

Section D · Grammar 3

Telling a short past story

Para narrar una vida o una experiencia, combina verbos en pasado con palabras de secuencia. Así el texto suena más claro que una lista de frases sueltas.

Sequence wordSpanish ideaExample
FirstprimeroFirst, she was born in Cádiz.
Thendespués / luegoThen she went to school.
After thatdespués de esoAfter that, she got a job.
Latermás tardeLater, she met her husband.
FinallyfinalmenteFinally, they bought a flat.
Rule

Age in past stories

Para la edad se usa be: She was 10, They were 20. No se usa have como en español.

Typical Spanish error

No traduzcas literalmente “tenía 10 años”: She had 10 years. → She was 10 years old. / She was 10.

Writing tip

En un texto A2, una buena historia puede tener 5–7 frases: fecha, lugar, dos o tres acciones importantes y una frase final.

Section E · Reading

A life in three photos

Lee el texto. Fíjate en las fechas, los verbos irregulares y las palabras que ordenan la historia.

A life in three photos

by Elena Ruiz

Photo 1 is from 1996. My grandmother Rosa was twenty-one. She was born on 14 April 1975 in a village near Granada. She grew up with three sisters, and she went to school by bus every day.

Photo 2 is from 2001. Rosa moved to Murcia because she found a job in a hotel. She met my grandfather there. They got married on 2 June 2003.

Photo 3 is from 2010. They had their first child, my mother, and later they bought a small flat. They didn't have much money, but they were happy.

Now Rosa lives near us. She tells great stories: first the village, then the hotel, and finally our family. I think every old photo has a small story inside it.
