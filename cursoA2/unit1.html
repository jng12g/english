People & Routines

Family, jobs and daily life. Aprende a hablar de quién es quién, qué hace cada persona y con qué frecuencia — con el present simple, los adverbios de frecuencia y los posesivos.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • talk about my daily routine and habits. — hablar de mi rutina y hábitos
  • describe my family and other people's jobs. — describir a mi familia y los trabajos de otros
  • say how often I do things. — decir con qué frecuencia hago las cosas
  • show possession with 's and possessive words. — expresar posesión

Section B · Grammar 1

Present simple

El tiempo de las rutinas, los hábitos y los hechos generales. I work. She works. They don't work. Es el tiempo más usado del inglés — domínalo bien.

B1 Affirmative — afirmativa

La forma base del verbo para casi todas las personas. La única excepción: la tercera persona del singular (he / she / it), que añade -s.

PersonVerbExample
I / You / We / TheyworkThey work in Yecla.
He / She / ItworksShe works in a hospital.
Typical Spanish error

No olvides la -s de la tercera persona. Es el fallo nº 1 de los hispanohablantes.
My brother works a lot.   My brother work a lot.

Spelling of the -s

EndingRuleExamples
most verbs+ sread → reads, live → lives
-ss, -sh, -ch, -x, -o+ eswatch → watches, go → goes, do → does
consonant + yy → iesstudy → studies, fly → flies
vowel + y+ s (normal)play → plays, buy → buys

B2 Negative — negativa

Se usa el auxiliar don't (I/you/we/they) o doesn't (he/she/it) + verbo base. Cuidado: tras doesn't, el verbo pierde la -s (la -s ya está en does).

PersonStructureExample
I/You/We/Theydon't + verbI don't work on Sundays.
He/She/Itdoesn't + verbHe doesn't work here.
Typical Spanish error

She doesn't work.   She doesn't works. — Una sola -s por frase.

B3 Questions — preguntas

QuestionShort answer (+)Short answer (−)

Does she work?Yes, she does.No, she doesn't.

Section C · Grammar 2

Adverbs of frequency

Para decir con qué frecuencia haces algo: always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never.

never0%
rarely~15%
sometimes~50%
often~80%
usually~90%
always100%
← less oftenmore often →
Position · la regla de oro

Antes del verbo principal, pero después del verbo to be.

• I usually get up at seven. (antes del verbo)
• She is always happy. (después de is)

Typical Spanish error

I always drink coffee.   I drink always coffee. — En inglés el adverbio va antes del verbo.

Section D · Grammar 3

Possessives & the genitive 's

Cómo decir de quién es algo: my, your, his, her… y el famoso apóstrofo 's.

D1 Possessive adjectives — adjetivos posesivos

SubjectPossessive adj.Example
ImyThis is my brother.
youyourIs this your car?
hehisHis name is Sam.
sheherHer job is great.
ititsThe cat eats its food.
weourOur house is small.
theytheirTheir kids are nice.
Careful!

El posesivo concuerda con quién posee, no con el objeto. La hermana de Luis = his sister (de él), aunque "sister" sea femenino. El error típico es decir her sister pensando en el género de "sister".

D2 The genitive 's — el genitivo sajón

Para personas, usamos 's en lugar de of. El poseedor va primero: «el coche de mi padre» → my father's car (literalmente "de-mi-padre coche").

SpanishEnglish
la casa de AnaAna's house
el trabajo de mi hermanomy brother's job
el nombre del perrothe dog's name
la habitación de los niños (plural en -s)the kids' room (solo apóstrofo)

Section E · Reading

A day in Sara's life

Lee el texto y fíjate en los verbos del present simple (resaltados) y los adverbios de frecuencia.

My everyday life

by Sara, a nurse from Murcia

Hello! My name is Sara and I'm a nurse. I live in a small flat with my sister, Marta. She's a teacher.

On weekdays I get up at six o'clock. I never have a big breakfast — I usually just drink a coffee. Then I go to the hospital by bus. My work starts at half past seven.

I often have lunch with my colleagues in the hospital café. My sister doesn't work near me, so we don't have lunch together. After work, I usually get home at five. In the evening I sometimes go to the gym, but I always read before I go to bed.

At the weekend, my life is different. I don't get up early! My sister and I often visit our parents. They live in a village near the sea. It's my favourite place.
