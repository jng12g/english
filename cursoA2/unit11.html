Rules & advice

En esta unidad aprenderás a dar advice, hablar de rules y construir frases sencillas con if: “If you study, you’ll improve”. Es una unidad muy práctica para viajes, trabajo, salud y vida diaria.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • give simple advice with should and shouldn’t · dar consejos sencillos
  • talk about rules and obligations with must and have to · hablar de normas y obligaciones
  • explain prohibition and no obligation · decir “prohibido” y “no es necesario”
  • make simple real-condition sentences with if + present + will · expresar consecuencias probables

Section B · Grammar 1

Should / shouldn’t

Should sirve para dar consejo. No es una obligación fuerte: es “sería buena idea”. La forma no cambia con I, you, he, she…

Rule

should + verb

Después de should usamos el verbo base: You should rest. Para el negativo: shouldn’t + verbo.

UseFormExample
Advicesubject + should + verbYou should drink water.
Negative advicesubject + shouldn’t + verbHe shouldn’t drive when he is tired.
QuestionShould + subject + verb?Should I call the doctor?
Typical Spanish error

No uses “to” después de should

You should to rest. → You should rest.

En español pensamos “deberías descansar”, pero en inglés should ya lleva la idea de “deberías”.

Section C · Grammar 2

Must, mustn’t and have to

Ahora pasamos de consejo a norma. Must y have to expresan obligación. Mustn’t expresa prohibición. Don’t have to significa que no es necesario.

Rule

Obligation, prohibition and no obligation

You must be quiet = es una norma fuerte. You have to show your ticket = hay una obligación externa. You mustn’t smoke here = está prohibido. You don’t have to pay = no es necesario pagar.

MeaningStructureExample
Obligationmust + verbYou must wear a seat belt.
External rulehave to + verbVisitors have to show a ticket.
Prohibitionmustn’t + verbYou mustn’t touch the paintings.
No obligationdon’t have to + verbYou don’t have to bring food.
Typical Spanish error

“Mustn’t” no significa “no tengo que”

You mustn’t pay. si quieres decir “no hace falta pagar”. → You don’t have to pay.

Mustn’t es “está prohibido”: You mustn’t smoke here.

Section D · Grammar 3

First conditional: if + present, will

El first conditional sirve para hablar de una consecuencia probable: si ocurre A, ocurrirá B. Es muy útil para normas, avisos y planes sencillos.

Rule

If + present simple, will + verb

If you touch the picture, the alarm will ring. También puedes cambiar el orden: The alarm will ring if you touch the picture.

If clauseMain clauseMeaning
If it rains,we will stay at home.Si llueve, nos quedaremos en casa.
If you study,you will pass.Si estudias, aprobarás.
If she feels worse,she will call the doctor.Si se encuentra peor, llamará al médico.
Typical Spanish error

No uses “will” después de if

If it will rain, we will stay home. → If it rains, we will stay home.

En la parte de if, usa presente simple: if you go, if she feels, if it rains.

Section E · Reading

A museum visit

Lee el texto. Fíjate en cómo se mezclan consejos, normas y consecuencias con if.

Saturday at the science museum

Reading · A2

Marta is visiting a science museum with her English class. At the door, visitors have to show their tickets. The guide smiles and says, “You should stay with the group, because the building is big.”

Inside the first room there is a big sign: “Please be quiet.” There is also a warning near an old machine: “You mustn’t touch this.” Marta wants to take a photo, but phones are not allowed in that room.

Her friend Leo feels tired. Marta says, “You should sit down for a minute and drink some water.” Leo says he is fine, but he listens to her advice.

Before they leave, the guide explains one more rule: “If you lose your group, you will find a member of staff near the exit.” Marta writes the rule in her notebook. She likes clear rules because they make the visit safer.
