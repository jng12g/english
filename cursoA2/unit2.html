Section B · Grammar 1

like / love / hate + -ing

Para expresar gustos sobre actividades, usamos un verbo de gusto + el verbo terminado en -ing. I like cooking. She loves dancing.

FeelingMeaningExample
love ❤️encantarI love reading.
like 🙂gustarI like cooking.
don't mind 😐no importarI don't mind cleaning.
don't like 🙁no gustarI don't like runing.
hate 😣odiarI hate waiting.
Typical Spanish error

Tras un verbo de gusto, el segundo verbo va en -ing, no en infinitivo con "to" como pensarías del español.
I like swimming.   I like swim.   (También existe I like to swim, pero en esta unidad practicamos -ing.)

Spelling of the -ing

EndingRuleExamples
most verbs+ ingread → reading, play → playing
verb + edrop e, + ingdance → dancing, make → making, ride → riding
1 vowel + 1 consonant (stressed)double the consonantrun → running, swim → swimming, sit → sitting

Section C · Grammar 2

can / can't — ability

Para hablar de lo que sabes o puedes hacer: I can swim. She can't drive. Es un verbo modal: fácil, porque no cambia con la persona.

FormStructureExample
Affirmativecan + base verbI can play the guitar.
Negativecan't + base verbHe can't swim.
QuestionCan + subject + verb?Can you cook?
Short answersYes, … can / No, … can'tYes, I can. / No, I can't.
Two golden rules

1. Sin -s en la tercera persona: She can dance. She cans dance.

2. Verbo base después (sin "to"): I can swim. I can to swim.

Section D · Grammar 3

play · do · go

Tres verbos para las actividades. En español dirías "hacer deporte" para casi todo, pero el inglés elige uno u otro según la actividad. Apréndelos juntos.

VerbUse it with…Examples
playdeportes de pelota, juegos e instrumentosplay football, play tennis, play chess, play the guitar
doactividades sin pelota / individualesdo yoga, do karate, do exercise
goactividades terminadas en -inggo swimming, go running, go cycling, go hiking, go dancing
Quick trick

Si la actividad acaba en -ing, casi siempre es go: go swimming, go shopping, go skiing. Si es un deporte con pelota o un juego, es play.

Section E · Reading

Tom's weekend

Lee el texto y fíjate en los gustos (like/love/hate + -ing), las habilidades (can/can't) y los verbos play/do/go.

What I do at the weekend

by Tom, a student from Galway

Hi! I'm Tom and I love my weekends. On Saturday morning I always go running with my dad. I can't run very fast, but I really like running outside.

In the afternoon, my friends and I play football in the park. I'm not a great player — I can't kick the ball very well! — but it's fun. After that, I usually go home and play video games. I love gaming.

On Sunday it's different. My mum does yoga and my sister Lucy plays the piano. She can play really beautiful music. I can't play any instrument, but I like listening to her.

In the evening, we often watch a film together. My sister loves horror films, but I hate them! I prefer comedy. It's the perfect end to the weekend.
