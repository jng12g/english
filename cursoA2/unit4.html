En esta unidad añadimos el tiempo del now: el present continuous. Aprenderás a decir qué está pasando ahora, a compararlo con rutinas en present simple, y a describir habitaciones, tareas de casa y ropa.

Section B · Grammar 1

Present continuous: actions happening now

Usamos el present continuous para hablar de acciones que están ocurriendo ahora o alrededor de este momento.

Rule

Form: am / is / are + verb-ing

I am cooking. = Estoy cocinando. La parte importante es doble: auxiliar am/is/are + verbo con -ing.

TypeExampleSpanish idea
AffirmativeI am cleaning the kitchen.Estoy limpiando la cocina.
NegativeShe is not wearing a jacket.Ella no lleva chaqueta ahora.
QuestionAre they doing the laundry?¿Están haciendo la colada?
Short answerYes, they are. / No, they aren't.Sí. / No.
Spelling

How to add -ing

La mayoría solo añade -ing: cook → cooking. Si acaba en -e, normalmente cae la e: dance → dancing. En verbos cortos como run o swim, doblamos consonante: running, swimming.

Typical Spanish error

No olvides el auxiliar

She cooking dinner. → She is cooking dinner. En inglés no basta con el verbo en -ing; necesitas am/is/are.

Section C · Grammar 2

Present simple vs present continuous

La Unidad 1 ya presentó el present simple. Ahora lo comparamos con el present continuous: rutina frente a acción en progreso.

TenseUseTime wordsExample
Present simplerutinas, hábitos, hechos establesusually, every day, on MondaysI clean the kitchen every day.
Present continuousahora, temporal, en progresonow, right now, at the moment, today, Look!, Listen!I am cleaning the kitchen now.
Rule

Let the time word guide you

Every day suele pedir present simple. Now o at the moment suelen pedir present continuous.

Typical Spanish error

No uses simple para acciones de “ahora”

Look! She cooks dinner. → Look! She is cooking dinner. Con Look! estás señalando una acción en progreso.

Section D · Grammar 3

Clothes: wear vs be wearing

Wear puede hablar de hábitos. Be wearing describe la ropa que alguien lleva puesta ahora.

MeaningExampleSpanish idea
HabitI usually wear jeans.Normalmente llevo vaqueros.
Now / todayToday I am wearing a suit.Hoy llevo puesto un traje.
QuestionWhat are you wearing?¿Qué llevas puesto?
Typical Spanish error

“Llevar puesto” no se traduce palabra por palabra

I have put a jacket. → I am wearing a jacket. Para ropa puesta ahora, usa be wearing.

Good to know

Some verbs are usually not continuous

Verbos como like, want, know y a menudo have expresan estado, no acción en progreso. Por eso decimos I like this jacket, no I am liking this jacket.

Section E · Reading

A family video call

Lee el texto y observa cómo se mezclan rutinas con acciones que están ocurriendo ahora.

Saturday at home

A short A2 reading · present simple + present continuous

It is Saturday morning and our family is on a video call. I can see everyone at home. My sister usually works in an office, but today she is sitting in the living room. She is wearing a green jumper and black trousers.

My brother is in the kitchen. He normally cooks only at weekends, and right now he is making breakfast. He is not wearing shoes because he is at home.

My parents are in the garden. They usually clean the house on Saturdays, but at the moment they are drinking coffee. My mother is wearing a blue dress, and my father is wearing an old jacket.

I am in my bedroom. I usually tidy up in the evening, but now I am making the bed. After the call, I am doing the laundry.
