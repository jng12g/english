Comparisons

En esta unidad aprenderás a comparar personas, lugares y objetos con comparatives, superlatives, as…as, too y enough. Es una unidad puente hacia B1: frases cortas, pero con más precisión.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • compare people, places and objects comparar personas, lugares y objetos
  • talk about the best or worst in a group hablar de lo mejor o peor dentro de un grupo
  • say two things are equal or different decir que dos cosas son iguales o diferentes
  • use too and enough in simple opinions usar too y enough en opiniones sencillas

Section B · Grammar 1

Comparatives: bigger, more interesting, better

Usamos comparativos para comparar dos cosas: A is bigger than B. La palabra clave es than.

Rule

Short adjective + -er / long adjective + more

Con adjetivos cortos: cheap → cheaper, old → older. Con adjetivos largos: comfortable → more comfortable, interesting → more interesting.

AdjectiveComparativeExample
smallsmallerThis phone is smaller than mine.
bigbiggerThe blue bag is bigger than the red one.
easyeasierThis exercise is easier than the last one.
expensivemore expensiveDublin is more expensive than my town.
goodbetterThis café is better than that one.
badworseThe weather is worse today.
Spelling

big → bigger: si el adjetivo acaba en consonante-vocal-consonante, a veces doblamos la última consonante. easy → easier: si acaba en consonante + y, cambia a -ier.

Typical Spanish error

No combines more con -er: more cheaper → cheaper. Y no olvides than: bigger that → bigger than.

Section C · Grammar 2

Superlatives: the biggest, the most expensive, the best

Usamos superlativos para elegir una cosa dentro de un grupo: the best café in town, the most expensive shop in the street.

Rule

The + superlative

Normalmente usamos the: the cheapest, the biggest, the most comfortable, the best, the worst.

TypeComparativeSuperlative
shortcheaperthe cheapest
short CVCbiggerthe biggest
-yeasierthe easiest
longmore expensivethe most expensive
irregularbetterthe best
irregularworsethe worst
Typical Spanish error

En inglés casi siempre necesitas the con superlativos: This is best option → This is the best option.

Useful pattern

Para lugares y grupos usa in: the best café in the city. Para una lista o categoría también puedes usar of: the cheapest of the three.

Section D · Grammar 3

Equal, too much, or enough

A veces no queremos decir “más” o “el más”. Queremos decir que dos cosas son iguales, que algo es excesivo, o que algo es suficiente.

Rule

as…as / not as…as

as + adjective + as = tan…como. My room is as big as yours. Para diferencia: This street is not as quiet as my street.

MeaningPatternExample
equalas + adjective + asThis bag is as big as that bag.
not equalnot as + adjective + asThis café is not as cheap as the other café.
excessivetoo + adjectiveThis coat is too expensive.
sufficientadjective + enoughThe room is big enough.
Typical Spanish error

En inglés enough va después del adjetivo: enough big → big enough. Y too no significa “también” aquí: too expensive = demasiado caro.

Mini contrast

This flat is too small = problema. This flat is small enough = es suficientemente pequeño para lo que quiero. El contexto manda.

Section E · Reading

A weekend comparison

Two days in Cork

Reading · A2 travel note

Last month, Elena visited Cork with her friend Sam. On Saturday, they stayed in a small hotel near the centre. It was cheaper than the hotel next to the station, but it was also noisier than Elena expected.

On Sunday, they walked to the English Market. Elena thought it was the most interesting place in the city. Sam liked the park more because it was quieter than the market.

They had lunch in a café. The soup was good, but the sandwich was better. Elena said, “This is the best sandwich of the weekend.” The coffee was nice too, but it was too hot at first.

At the end of the day, Elena was tired but happy. Cork was not as big as Dublin, but for her it was friendly, colourful and easy to visit.
