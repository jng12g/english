Section B · Grammar 1

Past continuous

Describe una acción en curso en un momento del pasado: lo que estaba pasando. At 8 o'clock I was watching TV. Se forma con was / were + verbo -ing.

Personwas / wereExample
I / He / She / ItwasShe was cooking.
You / We / TheywereThey were sleeping.
FormExample
NegativeI wasn't listening. · They weren't working.
QuestionWas she driving? · Were you sleeping?
Short answersYes, I was. / No, I wasn't.
Typical Spanish error

No olvides el auxiliar was / were.
It was raining.   It raining.  ·  They were running.   They running.

-ing spelling reminder

Most verbs + ing (watch → watching). Verb ending in -e → drop it (make → making). One vowel + one consonant → double it (run → running, sit → sitting).

Section C · Grammar 2

Past simple vs past continuous

La combinación que da vida a las historias: una acción larga de fondo (past continuous) que se ve interrumpida por una acción corta (past simple).

The rule

Past continuous = la escena, la acción larga de fondo. Past simple = el suceso corto que la interrumpe.

• While I was walking home, it started to rain.
• When the phone rang, I was sleeping.

Linker+ which tense?Example
whilepast continuous (acción larga)while I was reading…
whenpast simple (acción corta)…when the lights went out.
Watch out

Las dos acciones a la vez van en continuo: While she was cooking, I was cleaning. Una secuencia de hechos terminados va en simple: I got up, had breakfast and left.

Setting the scene

Las historias suelen empezar con el pasado continuo para describir el contexto: It was a cold night. The wind was blowing and it was raining. Luego llega el suceso en pasado simple: Suddenly, someone knocked on the door.

Section E · Reading

A strange morning

Lee la historia. Fíjate en cómo el past continuous pinta la escena y el past simple cuenta los sucesos.

The walk to work

a true story… more or less

Last Monday, the weather was terrible. It was raining hard and a cold wind was blowing. I was walking to work, feeling tired and a little nervous about a big meeting.

While I was crossing the bridge, I heard a strange noise. Suddenly, a man on a bike slipped on the wet road and fell. He wasn't badly injured, but he looked very surprised!

I helped him up while other people were calling for help. In the end, he was fine, and we both laughed. I arrived late to work — but I had a great story to tell.
