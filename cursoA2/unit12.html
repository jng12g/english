Section B · Grammar 1

Present perfect: have / has + past participle

Usa el present perfect para hablar de experiencias de vida cuando el momento exacto no es lo importante.

Rule

Form

Subject + have / has + past participle

I have visited London. · He visitado Londres. No dices cuándo: lo importante es la experiencia.

TypeStructureExample
AffirmativeI / you / we / they have + participleI have visited Edinburgh.
Affirmativehe / she / it has + participleShe has tried sushi.
Negativehaven't / hasn't + participleWe haven't seen that film.
QuestionHave / Has + subject + participle?Have you been to Ireland?
VerbPast simplePast participleExample
visitvisitedvisitedI have visited a museum.
gowentgone / beenHave you been to Paris?
seesawseenShe has seen that film.
eatateeatenThey have eaten Indian food.
meetmetmetHe has met my sister.
Typical Spanish error

No olvides el auxiliar

I been to London. → I have been to London.

En español puedes decir “he estado”, pero en inglés necesitas siempre have / has en este tiempo.

Section C · Grammar 2

Ever, never, just, already, yet

Estas palabras son señales muy frecuentes del present perfect. Cambian el matiz de la frase.

WordUseExample
everpreguntas sobre experienciaHave you ever eaten Thai food?
neverexperiencia negativaI have never flown alone.
justalgo acaba de pasarShe has just arrived.
alreadyalgo ya está hechoWe have already bought the tickets.
yetpreguntas y negativas: “ya / todavía no”Have you finished yet? / I haven't finished yet.
Rule

Position

Ever, never, just, already suelen ir entre have/has y el participio: I have never tried it. Yet suele ir al final: I haven't tried it yet.

Typical Spanish error

Yet no es already

I haven't finished already. → I haven't finished yet.

En negativas, para “todavía no”, usa yet, normalmente al final.

Section D · Grammar 3

Present perfect or past simple?

La gran diferencia: present perfect para la experiencia, past simple para el momento terminado.

UseTime wordsExample
Present perfectever, never, just, already, yet, in my lifeI have visited Rome.
Past simpleyesterday, last week, last year, in 2020, when I was tenI visited Rome last year.
Rule

Ask first: “Do I say when?”

Si dices when, normalmente usa past simple: I went to Dublin in 2024. Si no dices cuándo y hablas de experiencia, usa present perfect: I have been to Dublin.

Typical Spanish error

No mezcles present perfect con un tiempo terminado

I have visited London last year. → I visited London last year.

I have visited London. también es correcto, pero sin last year.

Mini tip

Been vs gone

She has been to Paris = fue y volvió. She has gone to Paris = está allí ahora o se ha ido allí.

Section E · Reading

A travel challenge

Three experiences before Friday

A short blog post · A2/B1 bridge

This week our English class has a small challenge: try three new things before Friday. I have already taken a photo of a famous bridge, and I have just uploaded it to the class page.

Marta has never eaten Indian food, so yesterday she went to a small restaurant near the station. She says the curry was hot, but very good.

Leo loves music. He has been to many concerts, but he has never played on a stage. On Thursday he is going to play one song with his cousin's band.

I asked our teacher, “Have you ever lost your passport?” She laughed and said, “Yes, I lost it in Lisbon ten years ago. I was very nervous, but I found it under my bed in the hotel.”
