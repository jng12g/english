Food & Shopping

Comida, bebida, cantidades y compras. Aprende a distinguir lo que se cuenta de lo que no, a hablar de cuánto hay, y a pedir y comprar con educación usando would like.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • name food, drinks and quantities. — comida y cantidades
  • use countable and uncountable nouns correctly. — contables e incontables
  • use much / many / a lot of / a little / a few. — cuantificadores
  • order food and shop politely with would like. — pedir y comprar

Section B · Grammar 1

Countable & uncountable nouns

La gran división de los sustantivos en inglés. Saber a qué grupo pertenece una palabra decide qué artículos y cuantificadores puedes usar con ella.

CountableUncountable
Meaningse pueden contar: 1, 2, 3…no se cuentan con número
Examplesan apple, two eggs, three bananasbread, rice, water, milk, sugar, cheese
Plural?Yes → applesNo → breads
a / an?Yes → an eggNo → some bread
Typical Spanish error

Muchas palabras incontables en inglés se traducen como contables en español.
some bread / a loaf of bread   a bread  ·  information / advice   informations / advices

a / an / some

a/an + contable singular: an apple. some + incontable o contable plural: some rice, some apples. any en negativas y preguntas: There isn't any milk.

Section D · Grammar 3

would like

La forma educada de decir quiero. Imprescindible para pedir en una tienda, un bar o un restaurante.

FormExampleEspañol
I'd like + nounI'd like a coffee.Quiero / Querría un café.
I'd like + to + verbI'd like to order, please.Querría pedir.
Would you like…? (offer)Would you like some cake?¿Quieres / Le apetece…?
Short answersYes, please. / No, thanks.Sí, por favor / No, gracias.
like vs would like

Do you like coffee? = ¿te gusta el café en general?

Would you like a coffee? = ¿quieres un café ahora? (ofrecimiento). No confundas: I like a coffee, please. → I'd like a coffee, please.

Section E · Reading

At the café

Lee la conversación. Fíjate en would like, los cuantificadores y los contables/incontables.

Are you ready to order?

a waiter and two customers

Waiter: Good afternoon. Are you ready to order?

Anna: Yes. I'd like a sandwich and a cup of tea, please.

Waiter: Of course. And for you, sir?

Ben: Would you like to share some chips, Anna? … OK, I'll have a salad and a bottle of water.

Waiter: Anything else? We have a lot of cakes today.

Anna: Oh, yes! I'd like a slice of chocolate cake.

Ben: Not for me, thanks. I don't eat much sugar.

Waiter: Perfect. So that's one sandwich, one salad, and one piece of cake. I'll be right back.
