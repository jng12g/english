Past lives (1)

En esta unidad dejamos el presente y empezamos a hablar del pasado. Aprenderás was/were, el past simple regular con -ed, y palabras de tiempo como yesterday, last year y ago.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • talk about where I was in the past — hablar de dónde estaba o cómo era antes
  • describe completed regular actions in the past — contar acciones terminadas con verbos regulares
  • use past time expressions — usar expresiones como yesterday, last year y ago
  • ask and answer simple questions about past lives — preguntar y responder sobre experiencias pasadas sencillas

Section B · Grammar 1

Was / were

Was y were son el pasado de am/is/are. Los usamos para decir dónde estaba alguien, cómo era algo o qué edad tenía en el pasado.

Rule

Choose was or were

I / he / she / it → was. You / we / they → were.

TypeExampleSpanish idea
AffirmativeI was at school.Yo estaba en el colegio.
NegativeShe wasn't at home.Ella no estaba en casa.
QuestionWere they happy?¿Estaban felices?
Short answerYes, they were. / No, they weren't.Sí. / No.
Typical Spanish error

En inglés no se dice “I had 10 years” para la edad. Di: I was 10 years old, no I had 10 years.

Section C · Grammar 2

Past simple regular: verb + -ed

El past simple sirve para acciones terminadas en el pasado. En verbos regulares, añadimos -ed.

Rule

Regular verbs

Para la mayoría de verbos: play → played, walk → walked, visit → visited.

Verb typeRuleExample
Most verbs+ edwatch → watched
Ends in -e+ dlive → lived
Consonant + yy → iedstudy → studied
Short stressed verbdouble + edstop → stopped
Pronunciation

La terminación -ed puede sonar /t/, /d/ o /ɪd/. En A2 no necesitas memorizar todo: escucha mucho y reconoce que no siempre se pronuncia como “ed”.

Typical Spanish error

El pasado regular no cambia con la persona. Di I played, she played, they played. No añadas una -s: she playeds.

Section D · Grammar 3

Negatives, questions and past time

Para negar y preguntar en pasado simple usamos did. Después de did o didn't, el verbo vuelve a su forma base.

TypePatternExample
Affirmativesubject + verb-edShe visited London.
Negativesubject + didn't + base verbShe didn't visit London.
QuestionDid + subject + base verb?Did she visit London?
Short answerYes, subject did. / No, subject didn't.Yes, she did. / No, she didn't.
Time words

Con pasado simple son muy frecuentes: yesterday, last night, last week, two years ago, in 2020.

Typical Spanish error

Después de didn't y Did, usa el verbo base. Correcto: I didn't visit. Incorrecto: I didn't visited.

Section E · Reading

A short childhood story

Lee el texto. Marca las estructuras de pasado: was/were, verbos con -ed y palabras de tiempo.

My first school

by Nora · A2 learner text

When I was a child, my family lived in a small village near the sea. Our house was old, but it was warm and friendly.

I started school when I was six. The school was small, and my teacher was very kind. My best friend Clara was in my class.

After school, we played in the garden and walked to the beach. We sometimes visited my grandmother because she lived next to the school.

In 2019, my family moved to a city. At first, I wasn't happy, but I started a new school and liked my new classmates.
