Section B · Grammar 1

there is / there are

El equivalente de hay. Usa there is para singular y there are para plural. There is a park. There are two museums.

SingularPlural
AffirmativeThere is a bank.There are two banks.
NegativeThere isn't a bank.There aren't any banks.
QuestionIs there a bank?Are there any banks?
Short answerYes, there is. / No, there isn't.Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.
Typical Spanish error

"Hay" es siempre igual en español, pero en inglés cambia con el número.
There are three shops.   There is three shops.

B2 some / any

The rule

some → en frases afirmativas (plural o incontable): There are some shops.

any → en negativas y preguntas: There aren't any shops. · Are there any shops?

Section D · Grammar 3

How much / How many

Para preguntar cantidades. La elección depende de si el nombre es contable o incontable.

Question wordUse with…Example
How manynombres contables (plural)How many museums are there?
How muchnombres incontablesHow much traffic is there?
Countable vs uncountable

Countable (puedes contar: 1, 2, 3…): a museum → two museums, a shop → shops, a person → people.

Uncountable (no se cuentan con número): water, money, traffic, time, information.

Prices

Para precios usa siempre How much…?: How much is a bus ticket? — It's two euros.

Section E · Reading

Finding the museum

Lee la conversación. Fíjate en there is/are, las preposiciones y las direcciones.

Excuse me…

a tourist and a local in a small Irish town

Tourist: Excuse me, is there a museum near here?

Local: Yes, there is. It's not far. Go straight on and take the second left. The museum is opposite the park.

Tourist: Great. And are there any cafés near the museum?

Local: There's a nice café next to the museum, and another one on the corner, in front of the library.

Tourist: Perfect. How much is the bus to the centre?

Local: Oh, you don't need a bus — it's only five minutes on foot!

Tourist: Thank you very much!
