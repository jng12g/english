Future plans

En esta unidad vas a hablar del futuro cercano con be going to y will. La clave no es traducir “voy a” o “haré” palabra por palabra, sino elegir si hablas de un plan, una decisión del momento o una predicción.

By the end of this unit, I can…

  • talk about future plans and intentions hablar de planes e intenciones futuras
  • make predictions and quick decisions with will hacer predicciones y decisiones rápidas con will
  • contrast be going to and will distinguir planes preparados y decisiones espontáneas
  • use future time expressions in simple arrangements usar expresiones de tiempo futuro en planes sencillos

Section B · Grammar 1

Be going to: plans and intentions

Usa be going to + infinitive para planes o intenciones que ya existen antes de hablar. Es el futuro más útil cuando dices qué vas a hacer.

Rule

Form: am / is / are + going to + verb

I am going to travel. · She is going to study. · They are going to book a hotel.

TypeStructureExample
Affirmativesubject + am/is/are going to + verbWe are going to visit Cork.
Negativesubject + am/is/are not going to + verbHe isn't going to stay at home.
QuestionAm/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb?Are you going to travel?
Short answerYes, subject + am/is/areYes, I am. / No, she isn't.
Good to know

También usamos going to cuando hay evidencia ahora: Look at those clouds. It's going to rain.

Typical Spanish error

No elimines el verbo be. I going to travel tomorrow. → I am going to travel tomorrow.

Section C · Grammar 2

Will: quick decisions and predictions

Will es muy corto y muy útil. Úsalo para decisiones que tomas ahora, ofertas, promesas y predicciones basadas en opinión.

Rule

Form: will + infinitive

I will help you. · She won't be late. · Will they come?

UseSignalExample
Quick decisionI decide nowThe phone is ringing. I'll answer it.
OfferI want to helpI'll carry your luggage.
PromiseI promiseI won't tell anyone.
PredictionI think / maybe / probablyI think it will be sunny.
Typical Spanish error

Después de will va el verbo base, sin to. I will to go. → I will go.

Section D · Grammar 3

Going to or will?

A veces las dos formas hablan del futuro, pero no transmiten la misma idea. En A2 puedes usar esta regla práctica: going to = plan o evidencia; will = decisión ahora u opinión.

MeaningUseExample
Plangoing toWe have tickets. We are going to fly tomorrow.
Evidence nowgoing toLook! He is going to fall.
Decision nowwillI'm tired. I will go home.
Opinion predictionwillI think the journey will be easy.
Time expressions

Usa palabras como tomorrow, tonight, next week, this weekend, soon o one day para situar el futuro.

Typical Spanish error

No juntes los dos futuros. I am going to will travel. → I am going to travel. / I will travel.

Section E · Reading

A weekend plan

Lee el texto. Hay planes preparados con going to y decisiones del momento con will.

Group chat: Friday evening

A short A2 reading · plans and decisions

Laura and Tom are at home. Tomorrow they are going to take the train to Galway. They already have the tickets, and they are going to stay in a small hotel near the station.

Laura looks at the weather on her phone. “It is going to rain in the afternoon,” she says. “Okay,” says Tom. “I will take my jacket.”

Their friend Ana sends a message: “I finished work early. I will meet you at the airport café tonight.” Laura answers: “Great! We are going to have dinner there at eight.”

Tom cannot find his passport. He checks his bag, his jacket and the kitchen. “Don't worry,” says Laura. “I think you will find it soon.” Two minutes later, it is under a book on the table.
